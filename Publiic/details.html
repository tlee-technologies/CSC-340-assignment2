Discover More About North Carolina's Wildlife

🌲 The Black Bear

North Carolina is home to one of the largest populations of black bears in the eastern United States. Found mostly in the mountainous and coastal regions, these incredible creatures are shy, intelligent, and play an important role in the ecosystem.

  • Diet: Omnivore – berries, insects, and small mammals
  • Habitat: Forests and wetlands
  • Interesting Fact: Can run up to 30 mph and climb trees easily.

🦌 The White-tailed Deer

This graceful animal is found throughout North Carolina, from forests to suburban backyards. They’re easily recognized by the white underside of their tails, which they raise when alarmed.

  • Diet: Herbivore – grasses, fruits, acorns
  • Habitat: Woodlands, fields, and residential areas
  • Interesting Fact: Can leap over 8 feet and run up to 30 mph.

🐢 The Eastern Box Turtle

A beloved reptile of North Carolina, the Eastern Box Turtle has a domed shell and can completely close itself inside when threatened. It is the official state reptile.

  • Diet: Omnivore – mushrooms, berries, worms, insects
  • Habitat: Moist forest floors and meadows
  • Interesting Fact: Can live over 50 years in the wild.

🦊 The Red Fox

Known for its intelligence and adaptability, the red fox is a clever predator that thrives in both wild and suburban environments across North Carolina.

  • Diet: Omnivore – small animals, fruits, insects
  • Habitat: Forests, farms, and neighborhoods
  • Interesting Fact: Communicates with more than 28 different sounds.

🐊 The American Alligator

Mostly found in southeastern NC, these ancient reptiles are a symbol of southern swamps and rivers. They can be intimidating but are vital to their ecosystems.

  • Diet: Carnivore – fish, birds, mammals
  • Habitat: Freshwater swamps, lakes, and marshes
  • Interesting Fact: Can survive cold snaps by holding their noses above frozen water.

🦉 The Barred Owl

This large owl is known for its haunting “Who cooks for you?” call. Barred Owls are common in North Carolina's forests and swamps and are active mostly at night.

  • Diet: Carnivore – small mammals, birds, amphibians
  • Habitat: Woodlands near water
  • Interesting Fact: Unlike many owls, they are sometimes active during the day.
